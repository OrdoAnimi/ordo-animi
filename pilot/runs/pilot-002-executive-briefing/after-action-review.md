# After-Action Review

Pilot: Pilot 002 - Executive Briefing

Status: Complete

## What Happened

The simulated executive briefing moved through the intended sequence: opening frame, cost challenge, timing challenge, business relevance challenge, lower-cost alternative, and close.

The user initially explained the technology context too deeply. After VALOUR preparation and rehearsal, the user led with the decision, recommendation, trade-off, and next action.

## What Worked

- The opening became shorter and clearer.
- The recommendation was visible early.
- Cost was connected to risk reduction.
- Timing was framed as a controlled trade-off.
- The lower-cost option was explained without dismissing it.
- The close identified sponsorship, funding alignment, and delivery checkpoint.

## What Did Not Work

- The first draft sounded like a technical backgrounder.
- The user initially used vague phrases such as "cleaner path" and "manageable".
- The business risk was not explicit enough until rehearsal.
- The user needed prompting to stop before offering architecture detail.

## Where Clarity Improved

Before VALOUR:

The user explained dependencies and constraints.

After VALOUR:

The user explained business risk, cost exposure, timing, and decision options.

Strongest improvement:

From:

"The recommended option gives us a cleaner path."

To:

"The recommended option costs more upfront, but it reduces operational risk and avoids a larger remediation cost later."

## Where Composure Was Tested

Composure was tested during cost challenge. The user initially defended the technical option. The improved response acknowledged the cost concern and reframed it as a risk trade-off.

## Feedback Received

Simulated executive feedback:

- The shorter opening made the decision easier to follow.
- The cost trade-off was clearer when expressed as risk reduction.
- The briefing felt more executive-ready when technical terms were removed.
- The close created a concrete next action instead of a general discussion.

## Confidence After Support

Ending confidence: 8/10

Reason:

The user now has a reliable briefing structure: decision, recommendation, trade-off, business risk, options, and next action.

Yes.
