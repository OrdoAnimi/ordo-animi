# Scenario Preparation

Pilot: Pilot 002 - Executive Briefing

Status: Complete

## Audience

- Executive sponsor: wants a clear decision and business rationale.
- Finance representative: wants cost exposure and timing clarity.
- Delivery executive: wants impact on commitments and dependencies.
- Risk or compliance representative: wants visible risk ownership.
- Technology executive: wants confidence that the recommendation is technically credible without needing full design detail.

## Stakeholders

Decision stakeholders:

- Executive sponsor
- Technology executive

Influencing stakeholders:

- Finance representative
- Delivery executive
- Risk or compliance representative

## Decision Required

Executives must decide whether to approve the recommended technology path, defer the decision, or request a lower-cost alternative with explicit trade-offs.

## Context Summary

The organisation needs to choose a path for a complex technology decision. The recommended option requires investment and disciplined delivery, but it reduces operational risk and avoids a larger future remediation cost. A cheaper short-term option exists, but it increases risk and may create rework. Deferring the decision preserves cash in the short term but extends uncertainty and may increase delivery risk.

## What Is At Stake

The decision affects business continuity risk, delivery timing, cost exposure, and executive accountability. A delayed or poorly framed decision may create future operational risk that is more expensive to correct.

## What Could Go Wrong

- The briefing becomes a technical walkthrough.
- Executives lose attention before hearing the recommendation.
- Cost is discussed without risk context.
- The user sounds attached to a technical solution rather than accountable for a business outcome.
- The meeting ends with interest but no decision.

## What The User Must Avoid

- Do not explain the architecture before the decision.
- Do not present all technical options equally.
- Do not use internal jargon.
- Do not imply the recommendation is risk-free.
- Do not ask executives to infer the next action.

## Key Message

The recommended option is the best business decision because it reduces operational risk, keeps timing manageable, and avoids a larger future remediation cost. It is not the cheapest option, but it is the most responsible option.

## Recommended Opening Frame

"The decision I need today is whether to approve the recommended technology path or ask us to proceed with a lower-cost alternative. My recommendation is to approve the recommended path because it reduces operational risk and avoids a larger remediation cost later. I will keep this to the executive frame: options, trade-offs, cost and timing impact, risk, and the next action."

## Questions To Ask

- Is the recommendation clear enough to sponsor today?
- Is the cost and timing trade-off acceptable?
- Is there any risk that needs a different executive owner?
- If approval is not possible today, what evidence is needed for a decision?

## Likely Pushback

### Finance

"Why should we approve the higher-cost option?"

Response option:

"The lower-cost option reduces spend now but increases the probability of rework and operational exposure. The recommended option costs more upfront because it removes that risk earlier."

### Delivery

"Will this change the timeline?"

Response option:

"It adds controlled work now, but it reduces the chance of a later stop or rework cycle. The timing impact is smaller than the likely delay if we defer the decision."

### Executive Sponsor

"What happens if we wait?"

Response option:

"Waiting keeps the immediate budget position unchanged, but it extends the risk window and makes the eventual remediation more constrained."

### Technology Executive

"Are we over-engineering this?"

Response option:

"That is the right challenge. My view is that this is not over-engineering because the recommendation is tied to business continuity risk, not technical preference."

## Closing Statement

"My recommendation is to approve the recommended path and record the decision today. The trade-off is higher near-term investment for lower operational risk and less future rework. If the group agrees, the next action is to confirm sponsorship, funding alignment, and the delivery checkpoint for the next review."

## Operator Notes

The briefing should fit into five minutes. If the user cannot explain the recommendation without a diagram, the answer is still too technical.
