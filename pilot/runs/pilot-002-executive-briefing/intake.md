# Intake

Pilot: Pilot 002 - Executive Briefing

Status: Complete

## Role

Technical leader responsible for briefing senior executives on a complex technology decision.

## Background

The user has strong technical credibility and understands the architecture, delivery constraints, risks, and operational implications. They are trusted by technical teams but need to communicate more effectively with executives who do not need implementation depth.

## Current Challenge

The user must brief senior executives on a technology decision involving a modernisation path, delivery timing, cost exposure, and operational risk. The recommendation is sound, but the user's draft briefing is too detailed and spends too much time explaining how the technology works.

## Upcoming Meeting

Meeting: Executive decision briefing

Audience:

- Executive sponsor
- Finance representative
- Delivery executive
- Risk or compliance representative
- Technology executive

Decision required:

Executives must choose whether to approve the recommended path, defer the decision, or ask for a lower-cost alternative with explicit trade-offs.

## Confidence Level

Starting confidence: 6/10

Reason:

The user is confident in the recommendation but unsure how to keep the briefing short enough for the executive audience. They are concerned that executives may focus on cost or timing before understanding the risk of inaction.

## Desired Outcome

The executives understand:

- the decision required
- the recommended option
- the business reason for acting now
- the cost and timing implications
- the key risk if the decision is delayed
- the next action required

## Main Concern

The user may over-explain the technical background and fail to connect the recommendation to executive concerns: business continuity, cost, timing, risk, and accountability.

## Selected Scenario

Selected VALOUR scenario: Executive Briefing

Reason for selection:

This scenario tests whether the user can turn technical confidence into executive clarity. The briefing must be shorter, more commercial, and more decision-oriented than a technical design review.

## Operator Notes

Primary pattern to watch: too much technical detail before the business decision.

Primary improvement target: options, trade-offs, recommendation, and next action in plain executive language.
