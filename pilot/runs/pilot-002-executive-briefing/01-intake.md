# Stage 01 — Context Intake

## User story

As the VALOUR pilot operator, I want to capture the user's role, leadership situation, and desired outcome so that every subsequent stage is personalised to their context.

## Pilot ID

PILOT-002

## User ID

SIM-002

## Role

Senior Solution Architect, cloud transformation programme at a mid-size financial services organisation.

## Organisation context

Regional bank undergoing a core infrastructure modernisation. The migration decision has been in review for two quarters. The executive team needs a recommendation before the next board cycle.

## Leadership situation

I need to brief the CFO and CIO on whether we proceed with lift-and-shift or re-platform for the core banking middleware. The CFO is focused on cost containment and risk. The CIO wants speed and capability. I understand the architecture thoroughly but I am not confident I can speak their language.

## Desired outcome

I want to walk out of that briefing having given them a clear recommendation with a financial risk framing — not an architecture lecture. I want them to leave the room knowing what we are recommending and why, without needing to ask a second meeting.

## Starting confidence

4 out of 10. Executive-facing conversations are significantly harder than technical governance conversations. I know the answer but I am not sure I can deliver it in the right register.

## Operator note

User: Senior Solution Architect, cloud transformation. Key risk: using architecture language with a financial audience. Baseline confidence: 4/10. This is a harder scenario than Pilot 001 by design — executive audiences require a different register and the user has explicitly identified the translation gap.
