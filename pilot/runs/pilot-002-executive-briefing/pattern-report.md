# Pattern Report

Pilot: Pilot 002 - Executive Briefing

Status: Complete

## Leadership Pattern

The user's core pattern is technical detail before executive relevance.

The user starts from the mechanics of the decision because that is where their expertise is strongest. Executives need the opposite order: business decision, recommendation, options, trade-off, risk, and next action.

## Primary Strength

The user has credible technical judgement and can identify the real constraints behind the recommendation.

This creates strong substance for an executive briefing once it is translated into business language.

## Primary Risk

The primary risk is losing executive attention before the recommendation is understood.

If the user spends too long explaining context, executives may focus on cost, defer the decision, or assume the recommendation is more complex than it is.

## Best Scenario Type

Best fit:

Executive or sponsor briefings where technical decisions need business translation.

Examples:

- funding decision
- risk acceptance briefing
- technology modernisation recommendation
- delivery trade-off briefing

## Hardest Scenario Type

Hardest fit:

Executive conversations where the recommendation is politically contested and the technical leader has limited authority.

Reason:

The user can explain trade-offs well after preparation, but may need more support when the problem is stakeholder alignment rather than technical clarity.

## Development Focus

Primary development focus:

Executive compression.

Specific behaviours:

- open with the decision
- state the recommendation early
- translate technical risk into business impact
- limit options to a clear comparison
- explain cost as a trade-off
- close with a named next action

## Coaching Note

"Executives do not need less intelligence from you; they need your judgement compressed into a decision they can act on."
