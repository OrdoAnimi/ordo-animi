# Stage 07 — Pattern Summary

## Pilot ID

PILOT-002

## User ID

SIM-002

## Scenario used

Briefing CFO and CIO on a cloud migration decision — lift-and-shift versus re-platform.

## Starting challenge

The user had a thorough understanding of the migration complexity but defaulted to architecture-layer language when facing a financial and business audience. Every attempt to answer the executive question included technical terms that required translation before the executive audience could act on the answer.

## Main improvement

The user moved from describing the migration architecture to framing the decision as a managed investment with quantified risk and a clear recommendation. By the end of the session, the user could lead with the recommendation and support it with business consequence language rather than building to the recommendation through technical explanation.

## Primary pattern

**Executive translation gap.**

The user has strong technical mastery and can explain the architecture clearly to a technical audience. When facing a non-technical executive audience, the user defaults to the language of their expertise (architecture, dependency, integration, technical risk) rather than the language of the audience's decision frame (investment, risk, consequence, recommendation).

## Primary strength

Deep understanding of the migration complexity, dependency chains, technical risk, and delivery controls. The user has all the substance needed to make a compelling executive case — the gap is not the substance, it is the translation.

## Primary risk

The user may default to architecture terms in an executive conversation. Re-platform, lift-and-shift, dependency chain, integration point — these are accurate and precise, but they require translation before an executive audience can act on them. Under pressure or time constraints, the user may skip the translation step.

## Next focus

Enter every executive briefing by establishing the financial and business consequence frame first. State the recommendation in one sentence. Name the risk in business terms. Name the mitigation in business terms. Then offer technical context only if the audience asks for it.

## Useful language

- "The recommendation is to proceed with the managed migration — we have the controls to contain the risk and the business case supports the investment."
- "The risk is not the technical complexity. The risk is the delivery timeline if we do not make a decision in this quarter."
- "This is an investment decision, not an architecture decision. The architecture is ready. The question is whether the business appetite for managed risk is aligned."
- "The phased delivery model gives you a decision gate at month six — you are not making a single point-in-time commitment."

## Usefulness score

8 out of 10. The loop produced a clear pattern identification and a usable set of refined language variants. The Preparation Brief and Language Refinement stages were the highest-value steps. The rehearsal surfaced the translation gap clearly.

## Operator note

Primary pattern: executive translation gap. Next focus: enter every executive briefing by establishing financial and business consequence framing before explaining the architecture. Pattern is distinct from Pilot 001 (Technical confidence without decision framing) — both involve a translation gap but at different layers of communication (governance decision frame vs. executive financial frame). This distinction validates the VALOUR scenario taxonomy.
