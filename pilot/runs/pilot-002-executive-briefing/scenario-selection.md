# Scenario Selection

Pilot: Pilot 002 - Executive Briefing

Status: Complete

## Selected Scenario

Executive Briefing

## Why This Scenario Fits

The user is not trying to win a technical debate. They need executives to understand a recommendation quickly enough to make or sponsor a decision.

Executive Briefing is the correct VALOUR scenario because it tests whether a technical leader can:

- reduce technical detail
- frame business implications
- make decision options explicit
- explain risk in executive language
- connect cost and timing to the recommendation
- close with a clear ask

## Scenario Pressure

The pressure comes from executive time scarcity. The audience will not tolerate a long technical walkthrough. If the briefing starts too deep, executives may interrupt, focus on cost without understanding risk, or defer the decision.

## Success Definition

The scenario is successful when the user can:

- state the decision required in the first thirty seconds
- explain the recommendation in one short paragraph
- compare options without deep technical detail
- connect risk to business impact
- explain cost and timing honestly
- ask for a specific next action

## Failure Pattern To Avoid

The scenario fails if the user:

- opens with technical architecture background
- uses implementation terms without business translation
- hides the recommendation until the end
- presents cost without explaining risk
- gives executives too many options without a clear view
- leaves the next action vague

## Operator Decision

Proceed with Executive Briefing as Pilot 002.
