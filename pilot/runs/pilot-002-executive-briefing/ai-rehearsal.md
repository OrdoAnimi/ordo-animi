# AI Rehearsal

Pilot: Pilot 002 - Executive Briefing

Status: Complete

## Role-Play Setup

The AI plays a senior executive group. The user plays the technical leader briefing a complex technology decision.

Rehearsal objective:

Keep the briefing short, business-led, and decision-oriented.

## Opening Prompt

"You have five minutes with senior executives. State the decision, recommendation, trade-off, and next action without going into technical design detail."

## User Draft Answer

"The technical issue is that the current environment has a number of dependencies and constraints, and the recommended option gives us a cleaner path. There are several components involved, and the team has assessed the integration, support, and delivery implications. The cheaper option is possible, but it has some limitations. I can walk through the architecture and the options if useful."

## VALOUR Feedback

The draft sounds credible but too technical. The decision is not clear, the business risk is delayed, and executives are being invited into the architecture before they know what they are deciding.

Improve by:

- naming the decision immediately
- giving the recommendation early
- translating technical risk into business impact
- making options explicit
- ending with the required action

## Improved Opening

"The decision today is whether to approve the recommended technology path or ask us to proceed with the lower-cost alternative. My recommendation is to approve the recommended path. It costs more upfront, but it reduces operational risk and avoids a larger remediation cost later. The next action I need is agreement to proceed, with funding alignment and a delivery checkpoint."

## Executive Challenge Questions

### Challenge 1: Cost

"Why are we choosing the more expensive option?"

User draft answer:

"The more expensive option includes work that addresses the dependencies properly and gives us a stronger platform position."

VALOUR feedback:

This is still technical and vague. Explain the business trade-off.

Improved answer:

"Because the lower-cost option saves money now but leaves a higher risk of rework and operational disruption. The recommended option is more expensive because it removes that risk earlier."

### Challenge 2: Timing

"Will this delay delivery?"

User draft answer:

"There is some extra work, but the team believes it can be managed if we plan it properly."

VALOUR feedback:

The answer lacks confidence and does not compare alternatives.

Improved answer:

"It adds controlled work now, but it reduces the chance of a larger delay later. The timing impact is smaller than the risk of deferring the decision."

### Challenge 3: Business Relevance

"Why should this matter to us at executive level?"

User draft answer:

"Because the technology decision has implications for supportability and future architecture."

VALOUR feedback:

Translate supportability into business consequence.

Improved answer:

"It matters because this decision affects continuity risk, future cost exposure, and whether delivery can proceed with clear accountability."

### Challenge 4: Lower-Cost Alternative

"What would we lose if we chose the cheaper path?"

User draft answer:

"We would lose some of the robustness and may need to revisit the design later."

VALOUR feedback:

Name the trade-off plainly.

Improved answer:

"We would reduce near-term spend, but we would keep more operational risk in the business and increase the chance of future rework."

## Rehearsal Outcome

The improved answers are shorter and more executive-ready. They connect the recommendation to business risk, cost, timing, and decision options instead of explaining the technical design.

## Operator Notes

The strongest improvement is the opening. It gives the executive group a decision frame before any detail appears.
