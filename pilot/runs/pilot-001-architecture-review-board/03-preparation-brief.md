# Pilot 001 Preparation Brief

## User story

As a VALOUR pilot user, I want a short preparation brief so that I can enter the rehearsal with a clear audience, decision, message, risk, and close.

## Acceptance criteria

- Audience is defined.
- Decision or outcome is clear.
- Key message is concise.
- Likely questions are identified.
- Closing statement is usable.

## Success criteria

The preparation brief is successful when the user can explain the situation and desired outcome in under two minutes.

## Situation frame

The architect is presenting a cloud integration design to an architecture review board. The design is viable but has delivery pressure, security considerations, and several trade-offs. The architect needs the board to endorse the recommended pattern or provide clear conditional approval.

## Audience

- Enterprise architecture representative
- Security architecture representative
- Delivery lead
- Platform engineering lead
- Business sponsor
- Governance stakeholder

## Decision or outcome required

The board must decide whether to endorse the proposed cloud integration pattern and allow delivery to proceed with documented mitigations and follow-up actions.

## Key message

The recommended pattern is the most balanced option because it meets the business timeline, preserves auditability, avoids unnecessary platform complexity, and keeps the main risks visible and manageable.

## Questions to ask

- Are any decision criteria missing?
- Which risk needs explicit acceptance before delivery proceeds?
- Are the proposed mitigations sufficient for conditional approval?
- What must be documented before the next checkpoint?

## Likely questions

Security may ask whether the integration pattern has enough control and auditability.

Enterprise architecture may ask whether the design creates future platform debt.

Delivery may push for approval without extra conditions because of timeline pressure.

The business sponsor may want a simpler explanation of the trade-off and business impact.

## Risks to avoid

- Do not dive immediately into implementation detail.
- Do not defend every technical choice as equally important.
- Do not allow the conversation to lose the decision frame.
- Do not leave the decision implicit.
- Do not close without confirming next actions.

## Recommended tone

Calm, concise, decision-oriented, respectful of challenge, and clear about trade-offs.

## Opening frame

The decision I am asking for today is conditional endorsement of the proposed cloud integration pattern so delivery can proceed while we document the remaining security and governance actions. I will keep this at the decision level first: recommendation, options considered, trade-off, risk position, and next actions.

## Closing statement

My recommendation is to proceed with the proposed pattern under the documented conditions. The trade-off is that we accept some short-term integration complexity to meet the delivery timeline while preserving auditability and keeping the main risks visible. If the board agrees, the next action is to record the conditions and confirm the follow-up security and architecture checkpoints.

## Operator notes

The preparation brief is strong enough for rehearsal. The main thing to test is whether the architect can keep answers short when challenged.
