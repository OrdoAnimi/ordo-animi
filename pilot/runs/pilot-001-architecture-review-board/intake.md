# Intake

Pilot: Pilot 001 - Architecture Review Board

Status: Complete

## Role

Senior Solution Architect in an enterprise transformation programme.

## Background

The architect is experienced, technically credible, and familiar with architecture governance. They are trusted by delivery teams for design depth, but they are moving into a stronger architecture leadership role where clarity, judgement, and decision framing matter as much as technical correctness.

## Current Challenge

The architect is preparing to present a cloud integration design to an architecture review board. The design connects several enterprise capabilities through a governed integration pattern. Delivery timelines are tight, security assurance is still active, and the board needs to decide whether the design can proceed with documented conditions.

The architect understands the design well, but their default pattern is to explain too much. When challenged, they tend to add implementation detail instead of restating the decision, trade-off, and risk position.

## Upcoming Meeting

Meeting: Architecture Review Board

Audience:

- Enterprise architecture lead
- Security architecture representative
- Platform engineering lead
- Delivery lead
- Business sponsor
- Governance observer

Decision required:

The board must decide whether to endorse the proposed cloud integration pattern so delivery can proceed with documented risk mitigations and follow-up checkpoints.

## Confidence Level

Starting confidence: 5/10

Reason:

The architect is confident in the technical design, but less confident in keeping the conversation at the right altitude. They are concerned that board members may focus on isolated implementation details and miss the recommendation.

## Desired Outcome

The review board understands:

- the recommendation
- the options considered
- the main trade-off
- the residual risks
- the conditions for proceeding
- the decision being requested

The architect wants to sound clear, calm, and commercially aware rather than defensive or overly technical.

## Main Concern

The main concern is losing executive attention. If the opening is too technical, the board may perceive the design as complex, unresolved, or poorly governed even if the underlying architecture is sound.

## Selected Scenario

Selected VALOUR scenario: Architecture Review Board

Reason for selection:

This scenario tests architecture leadership under challenge. It requires the user to translate technical judgement into decision language, handle challenge without becoming defensive, and close the conversation with explicit next actions.

## Operator Notes

Primary pattern to watch: over-explaining under pressure.

Primary improvement target: decision framing before design detail.

Baseline evidence: technically strong user with a clear risk of losing the room through excessive explanation.
