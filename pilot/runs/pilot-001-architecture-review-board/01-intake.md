# Pilot 001 Intake

## User story

As a VALOUR pilot operator, I want to capture the user's role, context, leadership situation, confidence, and desired outcome so that the pilot can start from a clear and usable architecture leadership scenario.

## Acceptance criteria

- User role and context are captured.
- One realistic architecture leadership scenario is described.
- Desired outcome is clear.
- Confidence baseline is recorded.
- Sensitive organisational detail is avoided.

## Success criteria

The intake is complete when the operator can select a scenario and write a short situation frame without needing more discovery.

## User details

User ID: SIM-001

Name: Simulated Pilot User

Role: Senior Solution Architect

Organisation context: Enterprise transformation program with cloud integration, security, delivery, and business stakeholders.

Industry or domain: Enterprise technology transformation.

Architecture background: Experienced solution architect with strong technical capability and prior exposure to architecture governance.

Seniority level: Senior individual contributor moving into stronger architecture leadership expectations.

## Leadership situation

The user is preparing for an architecture review board. The design involves a cloud integration pattern that must connect several business systems while meeting security, audit, and delivery timeline expectations.

The audience includes enterprise architecture, security, delivery leadership, platform engineering, and a business sponsor.

The decision required is whether the review board will endorse the proposed integration pattern and allow delivery to proceed with documented risks and mitigations.

The main risk is that the architect may over-explain the technical design, lose executive altitude, and fail to make the decision, trade-off, and risk position clear.

## Desired outcome

The review board understands the recommendation, the viable options, the main trade-off, the residual risk, and the decision required. The architect is seen as clear, calm, credible, and useful rather than defensive or overly technical.

## What could go wrong

The architect could provide too much detail, respond defensively to security challenge, fail to state the decision, or allow the meeting to become a technical debate rather than a decision conversation.

## Communication pattern

Most relevant patterns:

- Over-explaining
- Too technical
- Loses executive altitude
- Defensive under challenge
- Struggles to close decisions

## Baseline

Confidence before VALOUR, 1 to 10: 5

Why this score: The architect understands the design but is not confident the review board will understand the decision frame or accept the trade-off without the conversation becoming too technical.

## Operator notes

Likely scenario: Architecture review board

Key user risk: Technical correctness without decision clarity.

Key stakeholder risk: Board members may perceive the design as unclear, risky, or not sufficiently governed.

Recommended next step: Select the Architecture Review Board scenario and create a preparation brief focused on decision, trade-off, risk, and closing statement.
