# Pilot 001 After-Action Review

## User story

As a VALOUR pilot user, I want to review what happened after the simulated board conversation so that I can turn the experience into product learning.

## Acceptance criteria

- Outcome is captured.
- What worked is captured.
- What did not work is captured.
- Confidence after VALOUR is recorded.
- One improvement focus is identified.

## Success criteria

The review is successful when the user can name what changed, what still needs work, and what they would do differently next time.

## Scenario

Architecture review board for a cloud integration design.

## What happened

The simulated user entered the board conversation with a clearer decision frame. The answer moved from technical defence to conditional endorsement, trade-off, governance condition, and next action.

## Outcome

The simulated board had a clearer path to approve the direction conditionally rather than leaving the decision unresolved.

## Stakeholder response

The board would likely see the architect as more prepared, less defensive, and more decision-oriented.

## What worked

The preparation brief forced the user to name the decision, audience, trade-off, and closing statement before rehearsal. The language refinement turned a technical answer into a board-ready answer.

## What did not work

The rehearsal stage exposed a product gap: the pilot needs a cleaner way to generate realistic board questions and store multiple rehearsal turns without becoming too verbose.

## Where clarity improved

Clarity improved when the answer changed from “the design is technically sound” to “the recommendation is conditional approval.”

## Where cadence improved

Cadence improved by leading with the concern, then the recommendation, then the supporting controls.

## Where empathy was shown

Empathy appeared when the answer acknowledged the stakeholder concern before explaining the design.

## Where authority was shown

Authority appeared when the answer explicitly named the decision and the conditions for proceeding.

## What should change next time

The rehearsal workflow should include two or three stakeholder questions, not just one. It should also separate board-level responses from technical appendix detail.

## Confidence after VALOUR, 1 to 10

8

## Operator notes

The simulated pilot proves the basic VALOUR loop. The biggest near-term product need is a structured rehearsal workspace and an automated pattern report generator.
