# Language Refinement

Pilot: Pilot 001 - Architecture Review Board

Status: Complete

## Purpose

Convert technical or defensive answers into concise leadership language that helps the board make a decision.

## Refinement 1: Opening

Overly technical version:

"The design uses a governed integration pattern with cloud-hosted services, event handling, validation, monitoring, and security controls."

Executive version:

"The recommendation is to proceed with the proposed integration pattern because it balances delivery timing, auditability, and platform fit."

Decision-oriented version:

"The decision needed today is conditional endorsement so delivery can continue with recorded security and architecture checkpoints."

## Refinement 2: Security Challenge

Defensive version:

"The controls are in the design, and the team just needs to configure them properly."

Empathetic version:

"I agree the board needs confidence that the control evidence is clear before production."

Boundary version:

"The open issue is evidence mapping, not whether the design can support the required controls."

Decision-oriented version:

"My recommendation is to approve with a condition that security signs off the evidence mapping before go-live."

## Refinement 3: Platform Debt

Over-explained version:

"There are a number of reasons we could not use the broader platform refactor now, including timing, dependencies, and impact on other teams."

Shorter version:

"The broader refactor is cleaner long term, but it does not fit the current delivery window."

Trade-off version:

"We are accepting bounded integration complexity now to avoid a larger delay."

Decision-oriented version:

"I recommend proceeding because the pattern remains aligned with the target direction and the supportability risk can be managed through an architecture checkpoint."

## Refinement 4: Delivery Pressure

Uncertain version:

"The conditions should be manageable and probably will not delay the team much."

Clearer version:

"The conditions are narrow enough to manage inside the delivery plan."

Boundary version:

"I would not recommend removing the conditions because they are what make the approval responsible."

Executive version:

"The conditions protect the delivery timeline by reducing the chance of a later governance stop."

## Refinement 5: Closing

Weak close:

"I think this is the best option, and I am happy to take feedback."

Stronger close:

"My recommendation is to proceed with the proposed pattern under the documented conditions."

Decision close:

"If the board agrees, the decision to record is conditional endorsement, with security evidence mapping and supportability checkpoints as the follow-up actions."

## Operator Notes

The refined language is materially stronger because it is shorter and more explicit. It keeps technical credibility while making the decision easier for the board to record.
