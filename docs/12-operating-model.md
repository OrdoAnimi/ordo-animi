# VALOUR™ Operating Model

Version: 0.1
Status: Private operating model

## Purpose

This document defines how the first VALOUR pilot should operate before a full product platform exists.

The operating model is deliberately simple. It is designed for a small number of early users, manual delivery, and fast learning.

## Operating principle

The first product is not software. The first product is the repeatable behaviour loop.

The behaviour loop is:

Prepare.

Rehearse.

Perform.

Review.

Improve.

## Roles

### Founder / Product Lead

Owns the product vision, user interviews, pilot delivery, market positioning, and refinement of the method.

### VALOUR Operator

Runs the pilot process, helps users select scenarios, prepares AI rehearsal prompts, reviews outputs, and captures patterns.

In the first version, the founder may also be the operator.

### Pilot User

Brings a real architecture leadership situation, completes preparation, rehearses with AI, applies the preparation in the real world, and completes an after-action review.

### Advisor or Reviewer

Optional. Reviews product direction, market fit, funding narrative, or pilot outcomes.

## Pilot process

### Step 1: Intake

Capture the user's role, context, current leadership challenge, upcoming scenario, desired outcome, and confidence level.

### Step 2: Scenario selection

Select the closest matching scenario from the scenario library.

### Step 3: Preparation

Use the scenario preparation template to clarify audience, decision, risk, message, likely pushback, and language.

### Step 4: Rehearsal

Run an AI role-play based on the scenario. The rehearsal should challenge clarity, cadence, empathy, authority, and decision framing.

### Step 5: Language refinement

Improve the user's responses into shorter, clearer, more appropriate versions.

### Step 6: Real-world use

The user applies the preparation in the real meeting or conversation.

### Step 7: After-action review

Capture what happened, what worked, what failed, what surprised the user, and whether VALOUR helped.

### Step 8: Pattern snapshot

Identify one recurring leadership pattern and one recommended improvement.

## Delivery cadence

For the first pilot, run a weekly rhythm:

Week 1: Intake and scenario selection.

Week 2: Rehearsal and language refinement.

Week 3: Real-world use and after-action review.

Week 4: Pattern report and feedback capture.

## Evidence capture

Every pilot should create evidence.

Capture:

- User role
- Scenario type
- Starting confidence
- Preparation notes
- Rehearsal output
- Improved language
- After-action review
- Ending confidence
- User feedback
- Testimonial candidate
- Product improvement notes

## Data discipline

Only capture what is needed for product validation.

Do not collect sensitive organisational information unless required.

Do not include confidential client names or proprietary architecture details in pilot records unless explicit permission has been granted.

Where possible, anonymise examples.

## Quality standard

VALOUR must be useful in real professional situations.

A good VALOUR output should be:

- Clear
- Short enough to use
- Context aware
- Respectful
- Decision oriented
- Empathetic without being vague
- Authoritative without being harsh
- Practical enough for the next meeting

## Early product risks

### Risk 1: Too much philosophy

Users do not need the full Ordo Animi doctrine at first. They need help with a real leadership moment.

### Risk 2: Too much architecture jargon

VALOUR should understand architecture language but translate it into decision language.

### Risk 3: Too much founder dependency

The pilot can be founder-led, but the process must become repeatable.

### Risk 4: Too much platform thinking

Do not build a full platform until the pilot proves demand.

### Risk 5: Weak evidence

Feedback must be specific. Ask what changed, what helped, and what would make the user pay.

## First operating target

Run the first manual pilot with a small number of users and produce enough evidence to decide whether VALOUR should move into a repeatable paid pilot.
