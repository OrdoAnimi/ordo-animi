## Purpose

This document tracks early validation conversations for VALOUR.

The aim is to test whether architecture leaders recognise the problem, understand the offer, and would use or support the pilot.

## Validation goal

Speak with five architecture or technical leadership contacts first.

Do not try to sell too early. Listen for pain, language, scenarios, objections, and willingness to act.

## Discovery capture

For each call, capture:

### Contact

Name:

Role:

Organisation type:

Relationship:

### Pain signal

Did they recognise the problem?

What language did they use?

What examples did they give?

### Scenario signal

Which scenario mattered most?

What scenario was missing?

### Buyer signal

Would they use it personally?

Would they nominate someone?

Would their organisation pay?

Who would own the budget?

### Objections

What made them hesitate?

Was the offer unclear?

Was the audience wrong?

Was the scope too narrow or too broad?

### Next action

Pilot, intro, review materials, second call, or no action.

## Validation rules

Look for repeated language.

Do not defend the idea during discovery.

Do not over-explain Ordo Animi.

Do not widen the product based on one conversation.

Do not treat polite interest as validation.

Validation means the person recognises the problem and takes a next action.

## Validation decision gate

After five conversations, decide:

- Continue with architecture leadership wedge.
- Refine scenario focus.
- Adjust buyer language.
- Run one manual pilot.
- Seek advisor review.
- Pause and reposition.
