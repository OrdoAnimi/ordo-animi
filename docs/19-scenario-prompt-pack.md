# VALOUR™ Scenario Prompt Pack

Version: 0.1
Status: Private prompt pack

## Purpose

This document turns the VALOUR scenario library into reusable AI prompts for the manual pilot.

## Prompt pattern

Each scenario uses four prompt types: preparation, rehearsal, language refinement, and after-action review.

## Global preparation prompt

I am preparing for an architecture leadership situation.

Scenario: [insert scenario]

Audience: [insert audience]

Context: [insert context]

Desired outcome: [insert outcome]

Main risk: [insert risk]

Help me prepare by clarifying the decision, audience needs, likely questions, key message, tone, and closing statement. Keep the output practical and usable before a real meeting.

## Global rehearsal prompt

Simulate the stakeholder group for this architecture leadership scenario.

Ask one question at a time.

Review my clarity, cadence, empathy, authority, decision framing, and ability to stay at the right altitude.

After each answer, give concise feedback and suggest a stronger version.

Scenario: [insert scenario]

Audience: [insert audience]

Desired outcome: [insert outcome]

Known risks: [insert risks]

Begin with the most likely first question.

## Global language refinement prompt

Review my answer below.

Original answer:

[insert answer]

Improve it for an architecture leadership context.

Create four versions: short version, executive version, empathetic version, and decision version.

Tell me what was weak in the original answer and what changed.

## Global after-action review prompt

Run an after-action review for this architecture leadership situation.

Scenario: [insert scenario]

What happened: [insert summary]

What I said: [insert key points]

Stakeholder response: [insert response]

Outcome: [insert outcome]

Help me identify what worked, what failed, where I lost clarity or cadence, whether I showed empathy and authority, and what I should do differently next time.

## Scenario prompts

### Architecture review board

Preparation: Help me clarify the decision required, recommendation, options, trade-offs, risks, standards alignment, likely questions, and closing statement for an architecture review board.

Rehearsal: Simulate an architecture review board. Ask questions about risk, standards, business alignment, implementation feasibility, decision quality, and clarity. Ask one question at a time and give feedback after each answer.

Review: Did I make the decision clear, stay at the right altitude, handle questions cleanly, and explain the trade-offs well?

### Executive briefing

Preparation: Help me reduce a complex technology decision to business impact, recommendation, options, risk, cost, and decision required. Identify what detail should be removed.

Rehearsal: Simulate a time-poor executive briefing. Ask direct questions about recommendation, business impact, risk, cost, and decision required. Flag where I become too technical or unclear.

Review: Did I respect the audience's time, translate technical detail into business language, answer directly, and make the decision clear?

### Discovery workshop

Preparation: Help me define the workshop goal, questions, stakeholders, risks, facilitation plan, decision points, and outputs. Warn me where I may jump to solution too early.

Rehearsal: Simulate a mixed stakeholder workshop with unclear requirements and conflicting priorities. Help me practise facilitation, clarification, and control of the room.

Review: Did I ask more than I told, manage the discussion, capture decisions and unknowns, and avoid premature solutioning?

### Challenging a poor solution

Preparation: Help me separate evidence from judgement, name the risk, preserve trust, and suggest a constructive alternative.

Rehearsal: Simulate a delivery discussion where the current solution is being defended. Help me practise raising the concern without sounding dismissive or obstructive.

Review: Did I challenge the design rather than the person, offer a better path, stay respectful, and make the risk concrete?

### Security or risk pushback

Preparation: Help me identify the control concern, evidence, residual risk, mitigation, accountability, and decision required.

Rehearsal: Simulate a security or risk review. Ask questions about controls, auditability, residual risk, and accountability. Give feedback on whether my answers build trust.

Review: Did I listen before responding, clarify the risk, propose a practical next step, and preserve trust?

### Delivery conflict

Preparation: Help me acknowledge delivery pressure, clarify the architecture concern, identify compromise options, and protect the minimum viable architecture position.

Rehearsal: Simulate a delivery discussion under time pressure. Help me stay calm, practical, and outcome-focused.

Review: Did I acknowledge pressure, avoid sounding bureaucratic, identify a practical path, and protect the architecture concern?

### Architecture trade-off explanation

Preparation: Help me reduce the options, define decision criteria, recommend one option, and make the trade-off clear.

Rehearsal: Simulate a mixed business and technology audience. Ask me to explain options and recommend one. Flag where the trade-off is unclear.

Review: Did I recommend clearly, explain trade-offs without overwhelming the room, and connect to decision criteria?

### Strategy-to-architecture translation

Preparation: Help me identify the strategic intent, required capabilities, constraints, architecture decisions, and sequence.

Rehearsal: Simulate a business sponsor with a strategic goal but limited technical detail. Help me practise translating strategy into architecture without overcomplicating it.

Review: Did I connect strategy to capability, avoid technology too early, and create a useful decision path?

### First meeting with a new team

Preparation: Help me create an opening that establishes credibility, listens first, clarifies my role, and builds trust without over-positioning.

Rehearsal: Simulate a first meeting with a new team. Help me practise opening with authority, humility, and clarity.

Review: Did I listen before leading, establish role clarity, avoid over-explaining, and build trust?

### End-of-day handover

Preparation: Help me capture open loops, identify what can wait, name any work residue, and create a simple VITA handover plan.

Rehearsal: Act as VALLUM. Help me close work mode and hand over to VITA. Ask what remains open, what must be parked, and what personal action matters now.

Review: Did I close the loop, avoid carrying work into the evening, follow the personal plan, and recover enough to start clean tomorrow?
