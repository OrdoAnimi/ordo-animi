# Use Case Catalogue

Version: 0.1
Status: Private execution catalogue

## Purpose

This document records the shift from discovery to acceleration.

The founder has already developed the original VALOUR use cases, pilot cases, leadership situations, architecture situations, and AI operating modes through earlier work. The current repo does not need to rediscover the scenario universe from zero.

The next task is to consolidate and operationalise the existing work into a pilot-ready product.

## Product stance

We are not asking whether the VALOUR concept has enough scenario depth.

The founder has already provided the scenario base through prior product work, sprint planning, AI thread design, leadership modelling, architecture leadership work, and lived professional experience.

The execution task is to turn that material into a usable pilot flow.

## Core scenario groups

### Architecture leadership

These are the first commercial scenarios because they align with Velocity Architecture and the founder's strongest market credibility.

- Architecture review board
- Executive briefing
- Discovery workshop
- Challenging a weak solution
- Security, risk, or governance discussion
- Delivery conflict
- Architecture trade-off explanation
- Strategy-to-architecture translation
- First meeting with a new team
- Workday closure and handover into VITA

### Professional leadership

These are adjacent scenarios that can later expand VALOUR beyond architecture.

- First-time manager leading a team
- Poor performance conversation
- Delegation without micromanagement
- Time and priority management
- Strategy communication
- Conflict between team members
- Giving difficult feedback
- Apologising after a leadership mistake
- Rebuilding trust
- Handling pressure in senior meetings

### Personal operating layer

These scenarios belong primarily to VITA and later product expansion.

- End-of-day decompression
- Recovery after high-pressure work
- Personal routine and rhythm
- Health and movement reminders
- Social connection
- Family contact
- Relationship communication
- Loneliness management
- Shutdown routine
- Personal reflection

### Governing layer

These scenarios belong to VALLUM.

- Work-to-life mode switching
- Impulse interruption
- Overbuilding prevention
- Frustration checks
- Pride checks
- Avoidance detection
- Boundary enforcement
- Context correction

### Truth layer

These scenarios belong to VERITAS.

- Evidence versus story
- Assumption testing
- Projection check
- Feedback interpretation
- Reality testing after conflict
- Distinguishing incompetence from misalignment
- Distinguishing rejection from absence of contact
- Distinguishing confidence from overreach

## Commercial sequencing

The full catalogue is real, but it must not all be shipped at once.

The first commercial release stays focused on VALOUR for Architecture Leadership.

The other scenario groups remain available as expansion paths once the first wedge is working.

## Execution implication

Do not spend another cycle inventing scenarios.

Use the existing catalogue.

Move to productisation:

- Turn scenarios into pilot flows.
- Turn pilot flows into reusable prompts.
- Turn prompts into user experience steps.
- Turn user experience steps into the first lightweight product design.
- Turn product design into a build brief.

## Next action

Create the product backlog for the first VALOUR pilot workflow.

The backlog should translate the existing scenarios into epics, user stories, acceptance criteria, and release sequencing.
