# Risk and IP Notes

Version: 0.1
Status: Private founder risk notes

## Purpose

This document captures early risk and IP considerations for Ordo Animi and VALOUR.

It is not legal advice. It is an operating note to help protect the founder workspace, preserve provenance, and avoid avoidable exposure while the product is still forming.

## Core risk

The founder has previously shared parts of the original concept, prompts, architecture, and design thinking with external parties.

There may be related products or adjacent versions being developed elsewhere.

The response is not panic. The response is discipline.

## Operating position

Assume parts of the idea have leaked.

Build the version only this founder can credibly build.

Protect the deeper architecture from now on.

Ship a focused version faster than the full vision.

## Evidence folder

Create a private evidence folder outside the public product narrative.

Suggested folder name:

`archive/origin-evidence/`

Contents may include:

- Original prompts
- Early concept documents
- Shared files
- Email threads
- Chat exports
- Meeting notes
- Diagrams
- Repo commits
- Timestamps
- Any record showing what was created and when

Do not make accusations in this folder. Keep it factual and chronological.

## Sharing discipline

Use three levels of disclosure.

### Public pitch

Share the problem, audience, outcome, and high-level product definition.

### Funder or collaborator pack

Share market, roadmap, pilot, funding ask, and commercial logic.

### Confidential architecture

Share detailed system architecture, prompts, agent logic, and operating model only with trusted parties and appropriate written protection.

## What to avoid

Do not casually share the full Ordo Animi architecture.

Do not share detailed prompt systems without purpose.

Do not give unrestricted repo access to early advisors.

Do not accuse others without evidence and legal advice.

Do not let suspected appropriation stop execution.

## Practical protection steps

Maintain this private GitHub repository as a dated source of truth.

Use commits to create a development history.

Keep detailed origin documents in the archive.

Add copyright notices to public-facing materials when appropriate.

Use NDAs or written terms before sharing detailed architecture with potential partners.

Seek professional legal advice before making any formal IP claim.

## Strategic moat

The early moat is not only technical.

The moat includes:

- Founder-market fit in architecture leadership
- Velocity Architecture ecosystem alignment
- Lived prototype across professional and personal modes
- The Ordo Animi five-layer architecture
- Scenario-based architecture leadership focus
- Founder voice and public body of work
- Practical pilot evidence

## Risk framing

If another party builds a related version in another vertical, that does not end the opportunity.

It confirms the market pattern may be valuable.

VALOUR should stay focused on architecture leadership first, where the founder has the strongest credibility and most defensible entry point.
