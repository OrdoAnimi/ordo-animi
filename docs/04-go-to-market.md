# Go-To-Market Strategy

Version: 0.1
Status: Private GTM brief

## Purpose

This document defines the first go-to-market path for VALOUR for Architecture Leadership.

The goal is to validate demand with limited spend and strong founder-market fit.

## GTM principle

Start where the founder has credibility.

VALOUR should start in architecture leadership, where the founder has lived experience, existing assets, language, market access, and proof of work.

## First offer

Working name: **VALOUR Architecture Leadership Pilot**.

Duration: four weeks.

Format: founder-led, AI-assisted, scenario-based.

Outcome: users prepare for and handle real architecture leadership conversations with more clarity, empathy, cadence, and authority.

## Pilot structure

Week 1: Leadership profile and scenario selection.

Week 2: Meeting preparation and AI rehearsal.

Week 3: Real-world use and after-action review.

Week 4: Pattern review, improvement plan, and feedback capture.

## Pilot audience

Start with a small number of individual users or one small architecture team.

Early users include enterprise architects, solution architects, cloud architects, security architects, principal consultants, architecture practice leads, and technical leaders moving into advisory or leadership.

## First outreach channels

The first outreach should be direct, not paid advertising.

Use the existing professional network, LinkedIn, architecture and cloud contacts, recruiters, former colleagues, consulting contacts, MSP contacts, architecture communities, and Velocity Architecture ecosystem links.

## Outreach message

I am piloting VALOUR, an AI-assisted leadership rehearsal system for architects and technical leaders. It helps users prepare for architecture boards, executive briefings, workshops, stakeholder conflict, and difficult conversations before they happen. I am looking for early pilot users or one small architecture team to test the first version.

## Validation metrics

Track contacts, discovery calls, pilot users, completion rate, scenarios completed, user-reported improvement, feedback, referrals, repeat interest, and team interest.

## First proof target

Within 90 days, secure a small pilot group, capture usable feedback, and show that scenario rehearsal helped users handle real conversations better.

## GTM risk

The biggest GTM risk is over-explaining the entire Ordo Animi system.

The market should hear VALOUR first.

Ordo Animi, VITA, VALLUM, VERITAS, and VIA explain the deeper architecture, but the first buyer needs to understand the immediate problem and value.

## GTM discipline

Do not lead with the full life operating system.

Do not lead with wellness.

Do not lead with generic leadership.

Do not lead with a full SaaS vision.

Lead with a real pain: architects need to prepare for difficult leadership conversations before they happen.

## First public content themes

- Why architecture fails in conversation before it fails in documentation
- Why architects need altitude control
- How to brief executives without drowning them in detail
- How to challenge poor solutions without becoming the blocker
- Why architecture leadership requires empathy and authority
- The difference between technical correctness and stakeholder trust
- The architecture review board as a leadership moment
- How to run an after-action review after a difficult meeting

## Funnel

Awareness: LinkedIn posts, Velocity Architecture content, direct network outreach.

Interest: short landing page and founder explainer.

Conversion: discovery call or pilot invitation.

Delivery: founder-led VALOUR pilot.

Evidence: feedback, case notes, scenario improvements.

Expansion: team pilot, cohort, or platform build.

## 90-day goal

Prove that VALOUR has a real market in architecture leadership before building a full platform.
